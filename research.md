## Research Interests

My research develops principled computational frameworks that bridge physics-based models, statistical inference, and machine learning. I work at the intersection of:

- Computational imaging and inverse problems
- Bayesian inference and optimization
- Explainable AI through differential geometry
- Medical image reconstruction and analysis
- Privacy-preserving machine learning

## Key Research Contributions

### Plug-and-Play Priors (PnP)

The Plug-and-Play Priors framework enables flexible integration of powerful denoising algorithms as Bayesian priors in inverse problems. This approach has been:
- Adopted by 100+ research laboratories worldwide
- Implemented in Samsung Galaxy smartphone fingerprint sensors
- Applied to MRI reconstruction, electron microscopy, super-resolution imaging
- Awarded the 2020 SIAM Imaging Sciences Best Paper Prize and 2018 IEEE Young Author Award

**Key Publications:**
- Sreehari et al., "Plug-and-play priors for bright field electron tomography," *IEEE Trans. Computational Imaging*, 2016

### Consensus Equilibrium

A multi-agent optimization framework that enables modular integration of disparate sources of expertise in computational imaging problems. Led to a $1.2M NSF research grant.

**Key Publications:**
- Buzzard, Chan, Sreehari, and Bouman, "Plug-and-play unplugged: Optimization-free reconstruction using consensus equilibrium," *SIAM J. Imaging Sciences*, 2018

### Latent Space Geometry for Explainable AI

Using differential geometric tools (Fisher-Rao metric, information geometry) to diagnose failures in representation learning networks. Provides mathematical framework for distinguishing architectural versus data-driven failures.

**Recent Work:**
- Sreehari, Ramuhalli, and Liu, "Differential Geometric View of Information Flow in Neural Nets," *IS&T Electronic Imaging*, 2025

### Bias-Mitigated Face Recognition

Privacy-preserving face identification using generative neural networks. Awarded Wells Fargo Medallion for Most Innovative Patent (2020).

**Publications:**
- Sreehari and Santos-Villalobos, "Bias-mitigated face ID using secure generative transforms," *SPIE*, 2023

## Current Projects

### MRI Reconstruction with Foundation Models
Developing foundation models trained on raw multi-coil k-space data for MRI reconstruction that preserves physically-captured information typically discarded by conventional pipelines.

### Latent Space Curvature Decomposition
Using Fisher-Rao metric and information geometry to diagnose representation network failures, providing a mathematical framework for distinguishing architectural failures from data-driven failures.
